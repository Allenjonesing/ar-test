AR Trail Adventure

Six experimental approaches to GPS-anchored AR waypoint trails — each uses a different heading strategy so you can find which one stays locked for your device.

🗺️

v1 — Live Compass

Continuously applies the device orientation compass to the AR world root. Best accuracy when the device reports a stable, tilt-corrected heading.

Live Compass
🧲

v2 — Locked Compass

Captures the compass heading the moment tracking starts and never updates it again. Waypoints can't drift from phone tilt during a session.

Locked at Start
📡

v3 — GPS Bearing

Ignores the device compass entirely. Derives heading from the GPS movement vector after you walk 5 m — zero compass drift possible.

GPS Only
📍

v4 — Camera Anchored

Places every waypoint at the XR camera's current world position — no GPS-to-XR coordinate transform, no heading needed. Most accurate for relative position; heading calibration irrelevant.

Camera Position
〰️

v5 — Smoothed Heading

LERPs compass updates at a slow rate instead of snapping. Reduces sudden jumps when the phone tilts up to look at AR content.

Smoothed
🧭

v6 — No Heading

Sets XR world rotation to zero always — raw GPS local coordinates with no compass influence. Accurate only if you begin the session facing north.

No Heading
